Welcome

The Plumas National Forest is a 1,146,000 acre forest located in the Sierra Nevada mountain range. The forest consists of three Ranger Districts: the Feather River Ranger District on the west side of the forest, the Mount Hough Ranger District in the middle, and the Beckwourth Ranger District on the east side of the forest.

Every year, the Plumas National Forest hires seasonal employees to help manage the forest. One of the positions they hire for is wildland fire fighting.

Wildland fire fighting is a great job and many people turn it into a career. It is hard work, but very exciting. As a wildland firefighter, you get to work outside almost all the time. You'll get to see some of the most beautiful places in the nation; some places that people rarely ever go, if ever. You'll be working with a variety of equipment and tools, but don't worry if you don't know how to use them -- we'll teach you. But before you get all excited and apply, READ through this website very carefully -- you'll need to know a few things first.

This site exists solely to assist with recruitment for fire suppression positions on the Beckwourth District only.
