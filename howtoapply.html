Applying

Thank you for considering employment with the Beckwourth District. Please browse through all the information available on this site to give you a better picture of the available positions and the expectations of being a wildland firefighter. Applicants are strongly encouraged to contact the Superintendent or Captain of the respective crew they are applying to. If possible, stop by the station to meet the crew. You can find the address on the contact page.

Hiring is conducted annually with the first round at the end of January and a second round at the end of February, if needed. Open positions are for GS-03, GS-04 and GS-05 Forestry Technicians. Applicants are encouraged to apply at all levels, regardless of previous experience. (Note that GS-05 applicants must be FFT1 qualified and have completed S-290). You should have your application in before January 16th, 2018 to be considered for the first round. Every year, 200+ applications are received for one to three vacancies per crew. It is important that you take your time and build your resume to be competitive.

Applicants must be available for the entire season.

Do you have what it takes?

Requirements

You must be at least 18 years old to apply. You’ll also need some basic training, which you'd receive through classes. Many community colleges offer such classes. You should start with just the basics; you'll receive further training on the job and with experience.

Basic Courses:

  • I-100
  • IS-700
  • S-190
  • S-130
  • S-133
  • S-110


If you complete these classes, you’ll at least have your foot in the door. However, before you go out and enroll in any of these classes, can you hack being a wildland firefighter?

Personal Demands

This job is extremely physically and mentally demanding. You must be willing to go weeks without seeing family or friends. You will work long hours and may get little rest. Of course, you will see some of the most beautiful parts of America, and be paid for it, but this job isn’t for everyone.

Project Work & Physical Training

When you are not on a fire, you can expect to be training or doing project work.

Project work may consist of using chainsaws and hand tools to clear vegetation out of an area or constructing handline for a prescribed fire. You could also be helping out other aspects of the Forest Service, including timber, range, and prevention.

Wildland firefighters usually workout every day. This physical training, or PT, can occur in the form of running, hiking, or other physical activities. Runs can range from a couple miles to five miles; hikes can be around the same distance.

How to Apply

  1. Go to USAJobs.gov
  2. Click on "Advanced Search."
  3. Copy and paste the appropriate announcement number below for your desired grade level into the "Keyword Search" box.
  4. Click "Search Jobs."
  5. Click on the job description header.
  6. Click on the "Apply Online" button.

Job Types

Seasonal
Seasonal employment usually lasts from the beginning of May through October or November.

Apprenticeship
Before applying for the apprenticeship, it is recommended that you first complete a season or two with an engine module or crew. The apprenticeship is a career-path oriented at promoting an individual to all aspects of fire suppression.

What is Needed

Besides the basic fire classes, the only additional thing you need is a pair of boots. We recommend the following brands: White's, Nick's, or Drew's. These boots are built tough for the job that is being performed. All other gear will be issued to you, however, it is your responsibility to take care of that gear or you'll end up buying it.

Application Tips

  1. Be sure that you have up-to-date contact information for all previous supervisors and put their contact info on the line after your work experience. If we are unable to verify your employment and obtain references, your application will not be considered.
  2. References should be able to verify your employment, validate your experience, and provide insight into your work ethic, physical fitness, and attitude.
  3. IN YOUR OWN WORDS provide a brief description of your present and previous positions, including non-fire jobs. List bullet points for your specific duties, accomplishments, and awards.
  4. Include team sports, clubs, and extracurricular activities that show leadership and teamwork. List your exercise regimen.
  5. Include any additional awards and specific accomplishments beyond those listed in your work experience.
  6. List any additional applicable skills (e.g., chainsaw, welding, carpentry, computers, etc.).
  7. Be sure to include all required documentation (an IQCS Master Record must be attached to your application unless you are applying for a GS-03 position).
  8. Ensure you have the correct location checked on your online application.
  9. DO NOT overstate your skill set. Employees are expected to live up to their resumes.

Veterans

Veterans are encouraged to apply. They are already familiar with the structure of the forest service since it is similar to the military.

Some tips:

  • Ensure you attach all applicable documents, including your DD-214, as requested.
  • It is understood that references may be currently deployed; try to provide alternative references of past supervisors. Contact the crew superintendent (add link to contact page) if you foresee any issues with your references.
  • Be aware that physical fitness expectations are quite a bit higher than those of the military. Please do not rely on successful completion of basic training as a measure of your physical fitness capability and compatibility with the crew.
  • If you need help with the application process, feel free to call the district office at (530) 836-2575, or stop by in-person.
